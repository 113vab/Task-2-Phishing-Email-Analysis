# Task 2: Phishing Email Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this task was to analyze multiple phishing email samples and identify common phishing indicators used by attackers to deceive users into revealing sensitive information.

---

## Tools Used

* Email Samples (Text Format)
* Online Research
* GitHub

---

## Email Samples Analyzed

### Sample 1 – Fake PayPal Verification Email

**Sender:** [security-update@paypa1.com](mailto:security-update@paypa1.com)

**Subject:** Urgent: Your Account Has Been Limited

#### Phishing Indicators

* Spoofed email address
* Suspicious URL
* Urgent language
* Threat of account suspension
* Generic greeting
* Social engineering tactics

#### Potential Risks

* Credential theft
* Financial fraud
* Identity theft

---

### Sample 2 – Fake SBI Banking Alert

**Sender:** [support@sbi-security-alert.com](mailto:support@sbi-security-alert.com)

**Subject:** Urgent: Your SBI Account Has Been Locked

#### Phishing Indicators

* Fake banking domain
* Urgent verification request
* Threatening language
* Suspicious URL
* Generic greeting
* Fear-based manipulation

#### Potential Risks

* Banking credential theft
* Unauthorized transactions
* Financial loss

---

### Sample 3 – Microsoft 365 Password Expiry Scam

**Sender:** [admin@microsoft365-support.net](mailto:admin@microsoft365-support.net)

**Subject:** Password Expiration Notice

#### Phishing Indicators

* Fake Microsoft domain
* Urgent password reset request
* Account deactivation threat
* Suspicious URL
* Generic greeting
* Credential harvesting attempt

#### Potential Risks

* Account compromise
* Data theft
* Unauthorized access

## Common Phishing Characteristics Identified

* Spoofed sender email addresses
* Suspicious or fake URLs
* Generic greetings
* Urgent requests for action
* Threats of account suspension or deactivation
* Requests for sensitive information
* Use of social engineering techniques

---

## Security Recommendations

1. Verify sender email addresses carefully.
2. Avoid clicking on suspicious links.
3. Hover over links to inspect destination URLs.
4. Access services through official websites only.
5. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
6. Report phishing emails to the relevant organization.
7. Stay aware of common phishing tactics.

The analysis of three phishing email samples revealed common attack patterns used by cybercriminals, including email spoofing, malicious links, urgency tactics, and social engineering techniques. This task enhanced my ability to identify phishing attempts and understand the risks associated with email-based attacks.
